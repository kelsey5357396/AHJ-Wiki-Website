The Problem

Permitting friction is costing the industry — everywhere.

Every solar and storage installation requires approval from a local Authority Having Jurisdiction. There are over 36,000 AHJs across the U.S. — each with their own requirements, interpretations, and processes. That information is fragmented, hard to verify, and rarely documented in one place.

📄

Fragmented Information

AHJ requirements are scattered across PDFs, inboxes, and tribal knowledge. There's no single source of truth — and no standard structure.

⏱

Wasted Research Time

Designers and permitters spend hours tracking down requirements that should be findable in minutes. That's rework baked into every job.

🔁

Avoidable Rejections

Plan check rejections, costly corrections, and delayed interconnection — driven by information gaps that a better system would eliminate.

💡

Every installer is maintaining their own database. Why not share one?

Right now, every solar installer, engineer, and design firm in the country is painstakingly building and updating their own internal AHJ database — independently, in parallel, at enormous cost. There are 36,177 AHJs that may receive a solar permit or interconnection request. No single source of truth exists. AHJ.wiki changes that.
